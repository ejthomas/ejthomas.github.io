PhD Quantum Engineering

September 2019 – July 2024

University College London and Quantum Motion (London, United Kingdom)

  • Title: Automation and Scaling of Fast Charge Readout in Silicon Quantum Dots
  • Project focus was a fast platform for measuring new silicon quantum dot designs and evaluating whether they could form effective host structures for spin qubits
  • Charge readout was made faster with reflectometry; here, the resonator required (with large footprint) could be shared between quantum dots by using a multiplexer switch to connect each to the resonator in turn
  • Designed and assembled printed circuit boards (PCBs), made cryogenic measurements of quantum dots connected via a multiplexing switch to shared readout circuit and analysed data to benchmark speed and sensitivity
  • As well as investigating multiple discrete multiplexer parts separate from the quantum dot chip, the throughput was increased to 1024 dots in one experiment by using switching electronics integrated on the same chip as the dots
  • Contributed to design and implementation of automated control and analysis code for a measurement on this scale
